Before you begin — a note on steps 1 and 2

Steps 1 and 2 will be completed and handed over to you before you start. You'll receive login details for a new domain management account (with Ionos) that is set up in your name and ready to go. You won't need to do anything for these steps — they're included here just so you understand what's been done.

1
Your domain is transferred to your own account
Handled for you — no action needed Done for you

Your web address (your domain) needs to be moved out of a shared account and into an account that belongs entirely to you. This is done using a service called Ionos, which is simply a place that looks after domain names.

Once this is done, you'll be given the login details for your Ionos account. From that point, your domain is yours independently — no matter what website platform you use in future, the domain stays with you.

ℹ
What Ionos is Ionos is a domain registrar — it's simply where your domain name is registered and renewed each year. It has no bearing on what your website looks like or how it works.
2
Your existing settings are copied across
Handled for you — no action needed Done for you

Behind the scenes, your domain has some settings that make things like email work correctly. These are called DNS records — think of them as a set of instructions that tell the internet where to send visitors and emails for your domain.

These settings will be copied from the old setup into your new Ionos account, so nothing breaks during the transition.

✓
Your email is protected The records that make your email work (MX records) are carefully preserved during this process. Your email will continue working throughout.
Steps you'll carry out
3
Set up Squarespace and build your new site
You do this — take your time You do this

This is the main creative part. You'll sign up for Squarespace and build your new site at a temporary web address (something like yourname.squarespace.com). Your current site stays live throughout — there's no rush and nothing will break while you work.

  • Create a Squarespace account Go to squarespace.com and sign up. You can start with a free trial — you won't need to pay until you're ready to publish.
  • Choose a template Squarespace will offer you a range of starting designs. Pick one that feels close to what you want — everything can be changed later.
  • Recreate your pages Work through your existing site and rebuild each page in Squarespace. Use this as an opportunity to update any wording or photos you've been meaning to change.
  • Review everything carefully Check each page on both a computer and your phone before moving on to step 4. Once you point your domain across, this becomes your live site.
  • Upgrade to a paid plan when ready Before connecting your domain in step 4, you'll need to be on a paid Squarespace plan. The Personal plan is sufficient for most business websites.

Squarespace help resources Squarespace has extensive guides and video tutorials for every part of the building process. If you get stuck, their support is very good.

4
Connect your domain to your new Squarespace site
You do this — follow each sub-step carefully You do this

This step tells the internet to start sending visitors to your new Squarespace site instead of your old one. It involves updating a few settings in two places: Squarespace and Ionos. It looks technical but each sub-step is straightforward if followed in order.

!
Only do this when your new site is completely ready Once these changes go through (which takes a few hours), your domain will show the Squarespace site. Make sure you're happy with it first.
  • Start the domain connection in Squarespace In Squarespace, go to Settings → Domains → Use a domain I own. Type in your domain name and choose the option to connect it (not transfer). Squarespace will then show you some specific codes you'll need for the next step.
  • Copy the DNS records Squarespace gives you Squarespace will display a verification CNAME record and a second CNAME record. Note these down carefully — you'll need to enter them into Ionos in the next step. The verification code is unique to your site.
  • Log in to your Ionos account and open DNS settings Go to ionos.co.uk and log in with the credentials provided. Find your domain, then look for DNS or DNS Settings. This is where you'll add the records from Squarespace.
  • Add the two CNAME records from Squarespace In Ionos, add each CNAME record exactly as shown in Squarespace. Don't change any of the values. If Ionos already has a CNAME entry for "www", edit it rather than adding a duplicate.
  • Wait for it to take effect DNS changes can take anywhere from a few minutes to 48 hours to fully propagate. You can check progress back in Squarespace under Settings → Domains. When connected, it will show a green confirmation.

You're live — a few things to keep in mind
Ongoing
  • Renew your domain through Ionos each year Ionos will email you ahead of the renewal date. Keep your Ionos login details somewhere safe.
  • Your Squarespace subscription renews separately This is charged by Squarespace directly and is separate from your domain renewal.
  • You can update your site at any time Log in to squarespace.com to edit text, swap photos, or make any other changes whenever you like.
  • Your domain is portable If you ever want to move away from Squarespace in the future, your domain stays in your Ionos account and is not tied to Squarespace in any way.
